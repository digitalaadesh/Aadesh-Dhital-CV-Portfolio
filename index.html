The Plan

E-E-A-T Led SEO for Financial Services

The strategy centred on aligning Aspect's content with Google's E-E-A-T framework (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness) — the standard applied to all YMYL (Your Money or Your Life) content in financial services categories.

  • 1
    Restructure all product pages — Income Protection, TPD, Trauma, Accidental Death — with intent-matched headings, FAQs, and authoritative content demonstrating genuine underwriting expertise
  • 2
    Build author credibility signals — team page optimisation, professional credentials, and content attribution — to strengthen E-E-A-T signals at page level
  • 3
    Develop a Content Hub (Insights) targeting educational queries around insurance types, claims processes, and A&H product comparisons
  • 4
    Improve on-page technical signals — schema markup (Insurance Product, FAQPage, Organization), metadata, and internal linking — across all key pages
The Execution

Building Trust at Every Layer

Product Page Restructure

Each product page was rewritten to lead with clear, jargon-appropriate explanations of the insurance product, followed by eligibility criteria, claims process overviews, and FAQ sections targeting common search queries. Keyword targeting was aligned to how real buyers search — "income protection insurance quote", "TPD insurance Melbourne", "trauma cover Australia" — without sacrificing the professional tone the brand required.

Content Hub Development

A structured Content Hub (Insights section) was developed with articles targeting awareness and consideration-stage queries — "what is income protection insurance", "difference between TPD and income protection", "how to claim trauma insurance in Australia". These articles built topical authority signals and drew top-of-funnel traffic that could be converted via internal links to product pages and the quick quote tool.

Schema Markup & Technical SEO

Insurance Product schema, FAQPage schema, and Organization schema were implemented across the site — providing structured data signals that help Google understand the nature and credibility of the content. Metadata was rewritten across all pages with clear, intent-matched title tags and meta descriptions to improve click-through rate from SERP.

E-E-A-T Signal Building

The team page was restructured to highlight professional credentials, industry affiliations (McLardy McShane Partners, Steadfast Group, Lloyd's), and years of experience.
